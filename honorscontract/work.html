WORK

Welcome to New York City! Finding a job here can be challenging -but don't worry, we're here to help.

In this guide, we'll give you important tips and resources tailored just for newcomers like you. Whether you need help with finding work, understanding your rights, or improving your skills, we've got you covered.

Workforce Support

Workforce1 Centers help you find jobs, offer career advice, and teach you skills. Visiting one early can help you get a job faster.

Learn more at nyc.gov/workforce1 or call 311 for assistance.

Minimum Wage and Fair Treatment

Regardless of immigration status, all workers in the United States have certain fundamental rights, including:

Right to Minimum Wage: All workers are entitled to be paid at least the minimum wage as mandated by federal, state, or local law, whichever is higher.

Right to a Safe Workplace:Workers have the right to a safe and healthy workplace, free from hazards that could cause harm or injury. Employers are required to comply with Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) standards to ensure workplace safety.

It's important to know the lowest pay you can get and your rights at work. If you're not treated fairly, you can get help from the New York State Department of Labor.

Contact them at (888) 469-7365.

Education and Training

Going to classes can help you get better jobs. Look into programs at places like CUNY and the NYC Department of Education to learn new skills and improve your English.

-CUNY ENGLISH CLASSES -NYC DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION

Legal Help for Work

If you need help with immigration or work issues, programs like ActionNYC can give you free legal advice.

Call 1-800-354-0365 for assistance.

Protection from Discrimination

You have the right to be treated fairly at work, no matter who you are.

Right to Equal Treatment: All workers have the right to be treated fairly and equally in the workplace, regardless of their race, color, national origin, religion, sex, age, disability, or other protected characteristics.

Right to a Harassment-Free Workplace: Workers have the right to work in an environment free from harassment or hostile behavior based on protected characteristics. This includes protection from verbal or physical harassment.

If you're treated unfairly, you can tell the NYC Commission on Human Rights. Call 311 or visit nyc.gov/humanrights.

Rights as a Contractor

If you plan to work for yourself, know your rights about contracts and payment. Understanding these rules can help you avoid problems when working independently.

Contact 311 for "Freelance Workers" or visit nyc.gov/dca.
