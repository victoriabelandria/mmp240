WHERE TO LIVE

Welcome to New York City! Adjusting to life in the big city can be overwhelming, especially finding a place to live. We're here to guide you through the process.

In this guide, we'll give you important tips and resources tailored just for newcomers like you. Whether you're seeking assistance in securing accommodation, understanding your rights as a tenant, or navigating the rental market, we are ready to make your transition smoother.

Emergency Housing Assistance

Homebase is a city program that help you with temporary shelter, eviction prevention, and rental assistance services in case of emergency housing needs.

Contact NYC's Homebase program at (311).

Affordable Housing Lotteries

Participate in NYC's affordable housing lotteries through NYC Housing Connect for a chance to secure subsidized housing.

Visit housingconnect.nyc.gov for updates on available units and application deadlines.

Public Housing Assistance

Apply for affordable housing options provided by the New York City Housing Authority (NYCHA) online at apply.nycha.info.

Call (311) for assistance with the application process.

Housing Assistance Programs

NYC Housing Connect serves as a centralized platform where individuals and families can search for and apply to various affordable housing opportunities across the city.

Call NYC Housing Connect at (311) or visit their website housingconnect.nyc.gov for information on affordable housing options.

Tenant Rights

Learn about your rights as a tenant in NYC by calling the NYC Tenant Rights Hotline at (212) 979-0611 for free legal assistance regarding leases, repairs, and landlord disputes.

Legal Assistance

Seek legal assistance from organizations like Legal Services NYC at (917) 661-4500 if you encounter housing discrimination or landlord harassment for free legal representation and advice.
