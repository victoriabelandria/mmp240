HEALTH

Welcome to New York City! Adjusting to life in a big city can feel overwhelming, especially when it comes to health services.

In this guide, we'll share tips and resources specifically tailored for newcomers like your. Whether you're looking for help finding a doctor, understanding your health insurance, or accessing medical care, here's all the info you need to make your transition smoother.

Access to Healthcare

Regardless of immigration status or ability to pay, all residents in NYC can receive medical care.

NYC Care

A new program started in 2019 to ensure healthcare access for all residents, including those ineligible for public health insurance due to immigration status or financial reasons.

To learn more and to enroll, call 1-646-NYC-CARE (1-646-692-2273) or visit www.nyccare.nyc

Health Insurance Assistance

Free, in-person help is available in multiple languages to sign up for health insurance.

Call 311 or visit the NYC Department of Health website for assistance.

Public Health Insurance

Immigrants with lawful status or pending applications may be eligible for low-cost or free public insurance like Medicaid or the Essential Plan.

Public Hospitals and Community Health Centers

NYC Health + Hospitals provide low-cost medical care to all residents, regardless of insurance or immigration status. Emergency care is available to everyone.

Emergency Medicaid

Low-income individuals, regardless of immigration status, can receive emergency medical treatment and care through Emergency Medicaid.

Visit health.ny.gov for more info

Confidentiality

Your immigration status and personal information are protected by law. NYC Health + Hospitals respects your privacy rights.

For more information or assistance on healthcare services and resources in New York City, call 311 or visit the NYC Department of Health website at: NYC Department of Health
