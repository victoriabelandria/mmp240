Welcome to the Services for Recently Arrived Immigrants in New York City! Here you will find crucial information about Employment, Education, Health, Legal Services, Housing, and Finance help.

It's important to stay informed about the resources available to you as you build your future in the city. At the end of each category, you'll find a link to the main guide, "Resource and Referral Guide for Recently Arrived Immigrants," which offers more detailed information on various forms of assistance and support available to immigrants in NYC.

Remember, knowledge is key to empowerment and success in your new home.

ALL ABOUT WORK

“Workforce1 Centers” help prepare and connect qualified candidates to job opportunities in New York City. They also provide career services workshops and advice.

Learn More

WHERE TO LIVE

If you are about to become homeless, Homebase can help you stay out of a shelter. It offers services such as assistance to prevent eviction, obtaining public benefits, financial counseling, and more.

Learn More

EDUCATION

All children have the right to enroll in school, regardless of immigration status. Students who have not received a high school diploma have a right to attend school until the age of 21.

Learn More

LEGAL SERVICES

For free and safe immigration legal help, make appointments with ActionNYC and NYCitizenship to learn about legal options, apply for immigration benefits, and receive financial counseling in multiple languages.

Learn More

YOUR HEALTH

In New York City, all residents can get access to health care, regardless of immigration status or ability to pay. NYC Care provides health care access even for those ineligible for public health insurance due to immigration status

Learn More

FINANCES

Eligible New Yorkers can file taxes for free at NYC Free Tax Prep sites. This service offers one-on-one assistance with certified volunteers to help file taxes, including prior year returns or amended returns, and apply for an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN).
